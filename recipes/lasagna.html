Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that layers wide sheets of pasta with rich fillings to create a hearty, comforting meal. Traditionally, it features alternating layers of pasta, savory meat sauce made with tomatoes and herbs, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and plenty of melted mozzarella or Parmesan on top. Baked until golden and bubbling, lasagna offers a perfect balance of textures—soft pasta, velvety sauce, and gooey cheese—that makes it one of the most beloved comfort foods worldwide.

Beyond its traditional form, lasagna is incredibly versatile and can be adapted to suit different tastes and diets. Vegetarian versions often highlight roasted vegetables, spinach, or mushrooms, while modern twists may include seafood, chicken, or even plant-based proteins. Each variation retains the essence of lasagna: a layered, oven-baked dish that brings warmth and satisfaction to the table. Its ability to be customized while still delivering that signature richness is what keeps lasagna timeless and universally appealing.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef – 1 pound
  • Sweet Italian sausage – ½ pound
  • Medium onion – 1, chopped
  • Garlic cloves – 2, minced
  • Tomato sauce – 1 can
  • Crushed tomatoes – 1 can
  • Tomato paste – 2 cans
  • Water – ½ cup
  • Sugar – 2 tablespoons
  • Salt – 3 teaspoons
  • Italian seasoning – 3 teaspoons
  • Dried basil leaves – 1½ teaspoons
  • Black pepper – ¼ teaspoon
  • Fresh parsley – ¼ cup, chopped

Cheese & Pasta Layers

  • Mozzarella cheese – 2 cups, shredded
  • Whole milk ricotta cheese – 15 ounces
  • Parmesan cheese – ½ cup, grated
  • Large egg – 1
  • Lasagna noodles – 12 sheets

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.
